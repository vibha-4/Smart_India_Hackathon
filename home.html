Digital Assistant for KYC documents

KYC (Know Your Customer) verification is a mandatory process in India that requires individuals and entities to provide specific information and documents to verify their identity and address. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has mandated KYC norms for various financial institutions, including banks, non-banking financial companies (NBFCs), and other financial intermediaries. The primary objective of KYC verification in India is to prevent financial crimes, promote transparency, and ensure the integrity of the financial system.

What is the purpose?

Access to legal information and awareness is a challenge for a large population in India, especially for those who are not literate or are from marginalized communities. There is a need to provide a user-friendly and easily accessible platform for legal awareness. our aim is to Develop a digital assistant that can provide legal information and guidance to people in a user-friendly manner. The digital assistant should be able to converse in multiple languages and provide information in a concise and easy-to-understand manner. The platform should be accessible through various devices, including smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers. .

Know it first

The Constitution of India is a dynamic document that has undergone numerous amendments since its adoption in 1950. Continuous updating and amending the Constitution are essential to reflect the changing needs of a diverse and evolving nation. These amendments address various aspects, including social justice, governance, and federal structure. They require a rigorous process involving both houses of Parliament, ensuring that changes are made through a democratic and consultative approach. The ability to amend the Constitution underscores its adaptability and resilience in maintaining the values and aspirations of the Indian people.
